Research Focus:
The laboratory focuses on lignin, a core component of the plant secondary cell wall. We utilize model plants like Arabidopsis thaliana to investigate its biosynthesis and regulatory mechanisms, and to explore the role of lignin in plant growth, development, and plant-biotic/abiotic stress responses. Beyond studying the biological mechanisms of lignin biosynthesis and metabolism, the team is also dedicated to translating fundamental research findings into practical applications to enhance the quality and yield of forest, forage, and crop species.

Instructor
Xin Li

Research Focus:
Utilizing experimental materials such as birch, poplar, Manchurian ash, and ornamental kale, the research primarily centers on plant reproductive biology. It focuses specifically on the molecular regulatory mechanisms of self-incompatibility and interspecific hybridization incompatibility. The approaches include employing multi-omics methods to screen for key genes and signaling pathways controlling incompatibility; using gene editing and other techniques to elucidate gene function; and applying molecular biology methods to decipher the molecular regulatory mechanisms.
